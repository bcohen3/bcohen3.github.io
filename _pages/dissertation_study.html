Examining the Impact of Generative Artificial Intelligence on the Adolescent Educational Experience

A research team at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County, is recruiting students currently in grades 7 and 12 to be part of a research study. Additional information about the structure of the study can be found in the consent and assent forms, which will be provided to potential participants who are found to be a good match for the study.

What is the primary purpose of this study?

The purpose of this study is to gain a greater understanding of how generative artificial intelligence (generative AI) is impacting the experiences of middle- and high-school students with regard to both academics and social-emotional learning.

Who is the principal investigator conducting the study?

The study is being conducted by Ben Cohen, a PhD student in human-centered computing at UMBC who is co-advised by Dr. Edward Dillon and Dr. Tera Reynolds. This study is neither sponsored nor endorsed by any of the participating schools or their staff.

What are the research questions?

RQ1: (a) What are adolescents’ motivations for (not) using generative AI to assist with schoolwork? (b) What is the impact of generative AI use over time (for both schoolwork and non-school tasks) on adolescents’ critical thinking abilities?
RQ2: What are the attitudes of middle and high school teachers and their students toward the use of generative AI in an educational setting?
RQ3: What are best practices for middle and high school teachers regarding (a) generative AI usage policies in their classes and (b) generative AI use to assist with their professional responsibilities?
RQ4: What is the impact of generative AI use on the social and emotional development of adolescents over time?
RQ5: What safeguards can and should be put in place to minimize the social and emotional harms of generative AI use over time on adolescents?
RQ6: Do the answers to the preceding questions vary depending on sociodemographic variables?

Which students are being recruited?

We plan to recruit two student groups. Current seniors are the only high-school students who were in high school before the launch of ChatGPT, so we are hoping to survey and interview these students to get a sense of how ChatGPT changed their high-school experiences. In addition, we are recruiting current seventh graders to be a part of a longitudinal study that will follow them through tenth grade (i.e., two years of middle school and two years of high school).

How long is the study?

For current seniors, their involvement will end at the conclusion of the 2025&en;2026 school year; we anticipate concluding all activities by early to mid-May. For current seventh graders, their involvement will end at the conclusion of the 2028–2029 school year (i.e., the conclusion of their tenth-grade year). Your child may choose to discontinue participation in the study at any time.

Is my child's participation voluntary?

Yes, your child's participation is strictly voluntary.

Will my child or their school be identified by name in the study results?

No schools or students will be identified by name.

Are there any instances in which the researchers might be compelled to reveal information about my child?

Under Maryland law and University System of Maryland policy, all researchers conducting the study are required to make a report if they have reason to believe child abuse or neglect is occurring or has occurred in the past.
